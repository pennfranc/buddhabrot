About the Trajectories

The overall shape and intricate details of Buddhabrot arise thanks to the trajectories of points $c$ that tend towards infinity (and before their magnitude starts to increase exponentially\) of the Mandelbrot equation $$z_{n+1} = z_n^2 + c,\quad z_0=0$$ This prompts the question of what kinds of trajectories we get for different values of $c$. The tool on the left lets you explore not only trajectories going to infinity, but also converging or oscillating ones. $c$ is wherever the mouse points. The number of points displayed for each trajectory The Mandelbrot set is indicated roughly by by the dark red dots. The number on the top left indicates how many iterations $n$ of the Mandelbrot equation are necessary for $|z_n|>2$ up until $n=\text{max iterations}$. We get $n=\text{max iterations}$ if the sequence does not diverge or if $|z_n|>2$ only for $n>\text{max iterations}$. The length of the trajectory displayed is always equal to $n$.
